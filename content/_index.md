AI-assisted engineering is quietly becoming the memory, the planner, the operator, and the reviewer of your work. Every layer you hand over is a layer you can't easily take back.

Tessariq is built on a simple belief: your workflow, your artifacts, and your operational memory should not depend on the incentives of any single vendor. It keeps specs, evidence, and handoffs in open formats - Markdown, JSON, YAML - so workflows stay portable and agents stay replaceable.

Runs are detached by default, promoted through Git review, and backed by evidence you can inspect with standard tooling. If a run changes code, you should see what happened, why it happened, and how to reproduce it.

This is also a lab. We publish field notes on what works, what fails, and where safety boundaries break under real workloads.
